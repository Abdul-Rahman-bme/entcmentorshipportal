Introduction to Mentorship

ENTC Department's Mentorship Programme

The ENTC Department at the University of Moratuwa is excited to introduce a structured mentorship program aimed at guiding undergraduate students in their academic, research, and career journeys. This initiative connects students with experienced mentors from academia and industry, offering personalized guidance and support to help students thrive. As a mentor, your expertise will play a pivotal role in shaping the future of the next generation of engineers, researchers, and industry professionals.

Mentor

Why Become a Mentor?

Impact, Career Growth, Networking, Recognition

Mentoring offers alumni and industry professionals the opportunity to give back to the ENTC community by sharing valuable knowledge, experiences, and guidance. As a mentor, you will help students navigate their academic and professional paths, refine their skills, and guide them toward their career goals. Additionally, mentoring can enhance your own leadership, coaching, and communication skills, making you a more well-rounded professional. You will also expand your network by connecting with talented students and fellow mentors from diverse fields.

Expectations

Expectations from Mentors

Guidance and Time Commitment

As a mentor, you will be expected to provide career and research guidance, offer constructive feedback on student projects, and assist with the development of key professional skills. You will help students identify their preferred career path or research focus and support them in setting and achieving their goals. We recommend meeting with your mentees at least once every two weeks to ensure ongoing support and growth. Mentors will guide a group of 2-5 students, ensuring that you can provide tailored mentorship to each mentee based on their individual needs and aspirations.

Registration

How to Get Involved

Mentor Registration Form

Joining the ENTC Mentorship Program as a mentor is simple. To get started, please fill out the mentor registration form. Once registered, you will be invited to an onboarding session where you will learn more about the program structure, role expectations, and how you can best support your mentees. The session will provide you with the tools and knowledge needed to make the mentorship experience impactful for both you and your mentees.

Resources for Mentors

Mentor Support

As a mentor, you will have access to a variety of resources to support your mentorship journey. These include communication guidelines, best practices for mentorship, and a detailed onboarding presentation. You can also reach out to the program coordinators for additional support and clarification as needed. The goal is to ensure you feel equipped and confident in your ability to provide the best possible guidance to your mentees.
